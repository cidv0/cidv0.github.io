Fabrication Command Center

Crafting & Utility Stations

The workbench cluster is the heart of every safehouse. This guide shows how to progress through Abiotic Factor’s fabrication tiers, manage power-hungry benches, and arrange stations so the crew can prototype, cook, and heal without tripping breakers or blocking co-op workflows.

Field takeaway

Treat Station Level 1 ideas as a sprint: unlocking the Fabricator, Chemistry bench, and Kitchen suite within the first two nights keeps the team supplied and unlocks dozens of downstream recipes.1

Quick answer

Cluster the Field Workbench, Fabricator, and Chemistry Station around a central storage wall so raw materials only travel a few steps. Feed high-draw benches from a dedicated breaker, park fridges and medical gear on an uninterruptible line, and label permissions so specialists can enter their zones without gumming up the crafting queue.

Day 0–1 setup

  • Deploy the Field Workbench and stash a Tool Chest beside it for the passive crafting-speed buff.
  • Salvage microwaves, kettles, and hot plates from offices; even temporary cooktops stretch food supplies.2
  • Route a power strip behind the starter benches. Two low-draw appliances per outlet keeps the breaker calm.

Early progression

  • Complete the introductory idea chains to unlock the Fabricator and Chemistry Station; place them on opposite walls with shared storage between.
  • Install signage or floor tape so co-op partners know which bench handles metals, chemicals, or textiles.
  • Mount a Smart Battery near the kitchen appliances to ride out nightly brownouts.

Mid-game lab

  • Add the Electronics Bench, Medical Station, and Research Terminal in an annex with dedicated wiring.
  • Pair racks of labeled crates behind each bench so ingredients cycle in a single direction during production runs.
  • Stage a mobile cart with batteries and spare tools for field repairs between portal expeditions.

Station progression checklist

Move through the fabrication tiers in planned batches so unlocks arrive exactly when the base needs them. Use the table below as a reminder of what each station unlocks and how to stage it.

Station Unlock cues Power & footprint Best placement tips
Field Workbench Starter kit; assemble within the first shelter. No power draw; small floor tile. Anchor the crafting triangle; keep hand tools in adjacent locker.
Fabricator Complete initial Research Division ideas to unlock. Moderate draw; two wall outlets recommended. Face the storage wall so metal ingots move directly into the queue.
Chemistry Station Idea leads from solvent discovery; requires glassware. High draw; isolate on a strip or breaker. Keep near water barrels and fume hood ventilation (window or hallway).
Kitchen Suite Salvage microwaves, then craft Cooktop & Refrigerator. Steady draw; fridge prefers UPS backup. Arrange in galley layout with pantry racks within arm’s reach.
Medical Station Unlocked after first aid research chain. Medium draw during operation only. Place in quiet room next to chemistry for shared reagents.
Electronics Bench Electronics idea branch — requires copper wire, circuits. Burst draw; safe on shared strip with idle devices. Mount pegboard above it to hang components and schematics.
Research Terminal Story progression & captured data drives. Minimal draw; laptop-sized footprint. Seat next to idea board so clues and evidence share the same workspace.

Expect future content drops to introduce new tiered benches. Leave a few empty wall slots or modular floor space so upgrades slide into the existing electrical plan without rewiring the whole lab.

Power planning & wiring discipline

Benches cannot produce anything while breakers are tripped. Build redundancy into the grid before ramping up production.

Practical wiring rules

  • Respect outlet limits. Two low-draw devices or one major bench per wall socket keeps the line under the hidden amperage cap.3
  • Install Power Strips only when every connected device idles most of the day (terminals, lamps). Mix in one chemistry or fabrication bench and the strip will pop.
  • Run extension cords along ceiling hooks or cable trays so walkways stay clear for co-op traffic.
  • Add a Switch Box near the base entrance to kill crafting bays during raids without shutting down fridges.

Backup & automation

  • Pair high-priority devices (refrigerators, incubators) with a UPS or Smart Battery to ride out grid flickers.
  • Stage a Diesel Generator or Biofuel Turbine in a ventilated room with muffled walls; wire it into the grid via transfer switch to cover Anteverse excursions.
  • Label circuits using colored tape: blue for utilities, red for heavy fabrication, green for medical. Quick visual checks prevent misplugs.
  • Assign a team electrician who inspects cords nightly and tops off fuel before expedition days.

Workflow zoning & layouts

Smart floor plans shave minutes off every production run. Use these templates as a starting point and adjust for room geometry or team size.

Compact lab (3×4 room)

  • Workbench, Fabricator, and Chemistry Station hug three walls; storage crates stack between each bench.
  • A ceiling light and wall fan keep visibility high and fumes moving out the door.
  • Place the door-side floor clear so couriers can drop off loot without stepping into active crafting zones.

Modular R&D wing

  • Divide the hall into pods: fabrication bay, wet lab, kitchen, med bay. Each pod gets its own breaker and signage.
  • Install sliding partitions or bookcases between pods to dampen noise and keep chemicals away from food prep.
  • Center a logistics aisle lined with rolling carts so teammates can queue orders without hovering over benches.

Redraw layouts whenever new benches arrive. Capture the next draft on a whiteboard or tablet so every teammate knows where to drop components after portal runs.

Co-op permissions & roles

Station lockouts prevent ingredient theft but can slow production if left misconfigured. Revisit permissions after every major unlock.

Permission etiquette

  • Keep fabrication benches unlocked but post signage listing “Do not craft” items to avoid consuming rare parts.
  • Restrict Medical and Electronics benches to specialists to avoid half-finished orders clogging the queue.
  • Use colored floor mats to mark “waiting zones” so teammates can pass ingredients without crowding.

Communication tools

  • Mount whiteboards listing current projects, remaining ingredients, and assigned crafter.
  • Place storage with matching labels (“Metals”, “Organics”, “Medical”) behind the correct bench.
  • Set up a dropbox for completed items; couriers can collect gear without interrupting the operator.

Portable & field-ready options

Expedition kits keep the team productive when deep in the facility or across an Anteverse portal.

Travel essentials

  • Carry a packaged Field Workbench and a stack of idea cards so emergency repairs never wait for fast travel.
  • Pack a Compact Camp Stove and water kettle for on-site food. Refuel with wood, gas canisters, or biofuel.
  • Keep a charged Battery Pack and extension cord in the kit to power the Keypad Hacker or Survey Gear on demand.

Returning to base

  • Top off used batteries at the charging wall and log their status so the next squad knows what is ready.
  • Restock portable kits with spare wire, connectors, and fasteners pulled from the Electronics bench output.
  • Update the logistics board with any recipes unlocked on the expedition so the base crew can queue them immediately.
