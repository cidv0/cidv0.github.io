Habitation Planning

Furniture & Shelter Setup

Turning a reclaimed office into a livable habitat takes more than dropping a bed in the corner. This guide shows how to acquire furniture, arrange safe sleeping quarters, and reinforce entry points so nightly blackouts do not catch the crew without shelter.

Field reminder

Beds and packaged furniture retain durability when redeployed. Keep one screwdriver on the wall to package items fast after raids and reposition them before nightfall.1

Quick answer

Establish a bedroom with two beds and a safe-room door before the first blackout, place storage against walls to keep walkways open, and package any world furniture you like so it can move with the crew when you relocate.

Sleep logistics

  • Any bed lets a player set Home for respawns; place two so co-op partners are not forced to share timers.2
  • Craft a Makeshift Cot early, then replace with salvaged bunks for faster fatigue recovery.
  • Keep a spare cot packaged in storage for portable safe houses during expeditions.

Room roles

  • Divide your base into sleep, craft, and stash zones so clutter never floods the bedroom.
  • Use cabinets and lockers as room dividers; they offer cover while keeping gear within reach.
  • Mount lights or monitors near doors; outages are less deadly when you can see the barricades.

Mobility & upgrades

  • Package lounge furniture found in the facility; redeploy it at your base for free morale boosts.
  • Plan for future benches by leaving one wall empty for power strips and door reinforcements.
  • Label packaged sets (beds, lockers, decor) so moving day is a quick redeploy instead of a scramble.

Securing places to sleep

Safe bedrooms shorten fatigue recovery and guarantee a respawn point after surprise deaths. Prioritize an easily defendable room before expanding to craft wings.

Bed types & sourcing

  • Makeshift Cot: quick craft with cloth and scrap, ideal for prologue offices while you scout larger rooms.
  • Office Bunk: package from dormitories for better fatigue regen and shared spawn slots.2
  • Deluxe Bed: high-tier idea that pairs with powered lighting to boost comfort and faster wake-up buffs.

Bedroom layout

  • Place beds opposite the door with lockers forming a half-wall so incoming threats cannot swipe players while they sleep.
  • Hang a wall light or lamp behind the beds; the glow doubles as a visual respawn marker in co-op.
  • Stage a med cabinet and hygiene supplies near the exit so players can reset status effects after waking.

If a blackout hits mid-sleep, wake, swap in fresh batteries for lights, and re-arm door barricades before fatigue climbs again.

Packaging and relocating furniture

Most facility furniture can be packaged with the construction tool, preserving durability and repair state. Treat every office you clear as a showroom for future base upgrades.

Packaging basics

  • Equip a screwdriver or better tool, target the object, and choose Package to store it safely.1
  • World props can shatter if your Construction skill is low; use skill magazines before grabbing rare decor.
  • Packaged objects stack in inventory like other deployables, making relocation simpler than rebuilding from raw materials.

What to salvage

  • Grab filing cabinets and wall lockers for storage that fits the base aesthetic.
  • Lift couches, lounge chairs, and rugs to assemble morale zones on upper floors.
  • Package whiteboards and monitors to label rooms and prep quick-reference crafting recipes.

Relocation workflow

  • Sort packaged pieces into crates labeled Beds, Storage, and Decor so rebuilds follow a predictable order.
  • Deploy beds first to reclaim spawn points, then storage, then luxury decor to finish the room.
  • After redeploying, run a repair pass with scrap to top off durability lost during raids.

Keeping doors secure during blackouts

Entry control prevents surprise attacks while everyone is asleep. Treat barricades and furniture placement as a single system.

Barricade pairing

  • Stack filing cabinets perpendicular to the door to create a tight funnel for melee defense.
  • Place makeshift barricades or shelving behind the furniture so storms cannot knock the blocker loose.
  • Install a motion sensor or light stand aimed at the doorway for instant visual alerts when the power trips.

Emergency egress

  • Leave one pathway clear around the barricade — heavy furniture should slow invaders, not trap the crew.
  • Stage a pushcart with spare barricade materials (planks, screws) in the hallway for quick rebuilds.
  • Mount wall lockers near exits with emergency armor, flashlights, and batteries for rapid response.

Sample shelter layouts

Use these templates as starting points; adjust for room size and available doors.

Solo scientist pod

  • One cot, one locker, and a bedside light on a dedicated battery pack.
  • Desk repurposed as a crafting micro-station with a single crate for mission prep.
  • Door braced by a cart and filing cabinet combo; escape ladder or vent kept clear.

Four-player bunk wing

  • Two rows of bunks facing inward with a central aisle for gear carts.
  • Shared hygiene corner: sink, toilet, and towel rack so bathroom timers reset together.
  • Wall monitors at the entrance list nightly roles (watch, repair, scouting) to keep rotations smooth.

Draw floor plans on whiteboards and update them as you add benches; nothing ruins a night watch like tripping over a new locker in the dark.
