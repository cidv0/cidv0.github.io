Deployable Logistics

Furniture & Benches

Furniture turns empty offices into safe houses while benches unlock the crafting tree. This guide explains how to source deployables, progress through workstation tiers, and stage each room so the crew can rest, build, and store gear without slowing the campaign.

Field reminder

Packaged furniture keeps its durability when redeployed. Keep a screwdriver and spare fasteners on a wall hook so you can reclaim pieces after raids and rebuild the layout in minutes.1

Quick answer

Package office furniture for free beds and storage, craft benches in a Workbench → Electronics Bench → Medical Lab progression, and wire everything to stable power with clear floor space so nightly outages do not break production.1,2

Rest & respawn

  • Beds let every player set Home; keep at least one spare cot packaged for field deployment.1
  • Pair bunks with storage from the Inventory & Storage guide so night shift kits stay arm's length from the pillow.2
  • Place lights or monitors on independent batteries to keep respawn rooms visible during blackouts.

Bench priorities

  • Upgrade the Workbench first to unlock Packaging Station, Fabricator, and advanced kits.3
  • Electronics and Chemistry benches need grounded outlets; stage extension cords before deploying them.3
  • Keep one portable field bench packaged so expeditions can repair gear beyond the base perimeter or spin up an auxiliary Crafting Station.

Logistics loop

  • Anchor benches near labeled storage crates and carts from Inventory & Storage to avoid crafting bottlenecks.4
  • Assign permissions so specialists control power-hungry benches while others queue materials.
  • Mount signage showing recipes unlocked at each station to guide newer teammates.

Bench unlock milestones

Rotate through these milestones as you expand the workshop. Each tier adds new ideas and consumes more power, so plan walls, wiring, and storage beforehand.

1. Office salvage

  • Package desks, lockers, and bunks from the Office Sector with a screwdriver.
  • Craft a Makeshift Workbench and storage crates to anchor your first safe room.
  • Deploy a portable generator or tap a wall outlet to keep lights and chargers running.

2. Fabrication wing

  • Unlock the full Workbench to produce Packaging Stations, saw horses, and tool racks.3
  • Add a Field Kitchen or Camp Stove nearby to keep crafters fed mid-shift.
  • Stage carts and wall hooks so packed furniture rotates back into place quickly.

3. Electronics lab

  • Install the Electronics Bench and Battery Wall once wiring is ready for constant draw.3,4
  • Pair with dedicated storage bins for circuits, plating, and salvage cables.
  • Hang warning signage so teammates do not disconnect power mid-print.

4. Specialized bays

  • Add Medical, Chemistry, or Anteverse research benches in isolated, ventilated rooms.
  • Route UPS units and breakers so power spikes on one bench do not drop the entire wing.4
  • Keep redundant beds and lockers nearby in case evac forces a new forward base.

Layout & power best practices

Keep your benches energized and walkways clear so crews can work through nightly outages without injuries or lost output.

Segment power circuits

  • Give high-draw benches a dedicated breaker or battery chain so trips do not cascade across the base.4
  • Stage UPS units beside the Utility Stations hub to keep automation alive during grid drops.4
  • Label each outlet with painter’s tape so teammates know which battery or generator to service first.4

Protect traffic lanes

  • Keep carts parked perpendicular to benches so emergency evac routes stay open for stretchers and supply runs.
  • Add glow strips or lamps on independent batteries to mark pathways through blackout conditions.4
  • Mirror the safe-room layouts from Furniture & Shelter so crews instantly recognize rally points.

Schedule upkeep

  • Assign a maintenance slot each night to tighten bolts, restock screws, and rotate damaged furniture for repairs.1
  • Log consumables used per shift so your Inventory & Storage plan stays ahead of bench demands.
  • Mount whiteboards that list who packaged which room set so redeployments stay coordinated.

Sourcing furniture efficiently

Mix packaged decor with crafted staples to keep morale high and production humming.

Package what you find

  • Use a screwdriver to package chairs, bunks, terminals, and shelving from unsecured offices.1
  • Label crates by sector so redeployment crews know where each themed set belongs.
  • Repair worn furniture with scrap at the Workbench before redeploying to avoid mid-night breakage.

Craft bespoke setups

  • Use the Packaging Station to build signage, shelving, and barrier carts sized for your base.
  • Electronics Bench unlocks monitors, alarm systems, and lighting to bolster defenses.3
  • Combine crafted benches with world decor for morale and mechanical benefits without spoilers.

Co-op packaging rotation

Assign one teammate to sweep cleared sectors for packageable furniture while others escort the cart home. Rotate the role nightly so every player keeps tabs on what decor is available and which rooms need refreshes.

Room templates to copy

Start with these layouts, then customize based on sector hazards and available power.

Sleeper pod

  • Two bunks, personal lockers, and a bedside lamp on battery backup.
  • Door barricaded with a cart plus a quick path to bathrooms for timer management.
  • Wall board shows nightly watch rotation and evacuation route.

Fabrication lane

  • Workbench, Packaging Station, and tool wall spaced around a central cart bay.
  • Labeled bins for metals, wood, electronics, and chemicals flank each bench.
  • Ceiling lights on a shared switch separate from the rest of the base grid.

Operations pit

  • Conference table with map board, radio console, and monitor wall for alerts.
  • Emergency supply rack stocked with med kits, ammo, and batteries.
  • Adjacent secure door with barricade hooks and spare screw bins.

Take photos or whiteboard sketches of each room after major upgrades so returning teammates know where gear moved.
