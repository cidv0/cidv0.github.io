Logistics Reference

Inventory & Storage

Managing weight, slots, and stash locations is the difference between a smooth supply chain and a frantic dump of loot on the floor. This guide covers encumbrance thresholds, backpack upgrades, and how to build reliable storage wings for solo and co-op runs without leaking spoilers.

Field reminder

Pocket inventory starts at 12 slots; every backpack or belt you equip extends it. Stage one empty locker near each bench so crafted items never block your carry weight.1

Quick answer

Keep weight under the orange bar, upgrade to a backpack before leaving the Office Sector, and pre-label storage so co-op teammates know exactly where to dump loot.

Weight thresholds

  • Stay below Heavy Load (yellow) to avoid the 10% speed hit and stamina drain.2
  • Encumbered (orange) slows you by 30%; stash items before it sticks mid-fight.3
  • Overencumbered (red) bricks stamina regen entirely — you will crawl home.4

First upgrades

  • Grab the Basic Backpack in the Data Farm manager’s office; the duffel on the catwalk behind it adds even more slots.5
  • Pair a backpack with a fanny pack or utility belt to turn pockets into crafting component slots.
  • Stash a Pushcart or Laundry Cart near resource-rich hallways to shuttle scrap back safely.

Base logistics

  • Use color-coded signage (paintings, wall monitors) to reserve lockers per material family.
  • Split storage into Inbound, Crafting Output, and Mission Prep.
  • Review shelves weekly; recycle overflow into the Scrap Compactor or vending terminals.

Understanding carry weight & strength

The inventory screen shows a segmented bar: green pockets, yellow heavy load, orange encumbered, and red overencumbered. Each Strength level extends the safe zone slightly, so hauling heavy gear while buffed with protein shakes or the Strength stim remains the fastest leveling method.

Encumbrance cheat sheet

Heavy Load
-10% move speed, -25% stamina regen.2
Encumbered
-30% move speed, -50% stamina regen.3
Overencumbered
-75% move speed, stamina regen halted.4

You still build Strength XP while slogging through any penalty tier; use that to your advantage when a run ends near home base.

Tips to stay mobile

  • Favor stackable resources (wire spools, fabric rolls) over bulky single items until you have carts.
  • Drop filled specimen jars or bricks into nearby lockers before combat; they can be recovered later.
  • Share the load: in co-op, assign one player as the mule with highest Strength and the best backpack.
  • Keep a repair bench near storage so damaged armor doesn’t secretly weigh you down.

Backpacks, belts, and portable storage

Lootable containers appear all across the Office and Data Farm sectors. Prioritize anything that increases slot count, then branch into crafted or wheeled options once you set up a base.

Personal gear progression

  • Basic Backpack: Found in the Data Farm manager’s office, +8 slots and light weight.5
  • Duffel Bag / Tactical Pack: Common in security rooms; heavier but resists damage better.
  • Utility Belt / Fanny Pack: Occupies belt slot, holding small tools and freeing pockets for ammo.
  • Specimen Cart Harness: Crafted mid-game, adds dedicated slots for jars and research samples.

Portable containers

  • Laundry Cart / Pushcart: Wheel it along hallways; park it by elevators before clearing floors.
  • Cooler Chest: Keeps food fresh, ideal near kitchens or hydroponics harvest points.
  • Spare Backpacks: Drop a filled pack on the ground as a pseudo-cache before boss rooms.
  • Loot Spill Bags: Created when a teammate dies; mark them with glowsticks until recovered.

Upgrade milestones

  1. Secure the Basic Backpack and a belt item before nightfall on Day 1.
  2. Craft a Tool Bag once ideation unlocks fabric recipes; dedicate it to repair kits and wires.
  3. Build or steal two carts for group play — one for raw salvage, one for crafted deliveries.
  4. Transition to armored backpacks once you unlock riot gear to keep your carry solution from breaking.

Designing base storage wings

Treat storage like a factory floor: materials in, processing in the middle, mission kits out. The facility rewards methodical layouts, especially when mutants and bots crash your carefully scheduled nights.

Room blueprint

  • Inbound lane: Place lockers beside the entrance; stack heavy scrap low to avoid physics spills.
  • Crafting triangle: Bench, storage, power outlet within a few steps for fast refills.
  • Mission rack: Weapon board, med cabinet, and ammo crate staged near the exit door.
  • Leave a free tile for carts so co-op teams can park them without blocking pathing.

Labeling & permissions

  • Use wall monitors or whiteboards to document what belongs in each locker.
  • Assign storage ownership on private servers to prevent accidental dismantling.
  • Adopt a “two-stack” rule: if a bin overflows twice, promote that resource to its own locker.
  • Keep emergency kits (duct tape, spare armor, food) on a separate shelf with glowstick lighting.

Co-op workflow

Schedule nightly “drop-and-sort” sessions. One player reads materials aloud, the rest slot them away. End every run by topping off ammo crates and topping up mission packs so emergency response is instant.

Looping your supply chain

Map every salvage trip from pickup to deployment. Having a clear pipeline keeps ideation queues alive and prevents wasteful backtracking when nightfall hits.

1. Gather

Raid offices, laboratory storage, and maintenance closets with a cart staged at the elevator.

2. Stage

Dump haul into inbound lockers, sort immediately to avoid future clutter, and log rare drops.

3. Craft

Route materials to benches in batches, then place finished goods into mission-prep crates.

4. Deploy

Load backpacks with mission kits, mark spare carts on the map, and rotate crews for the next run.
