Utility Tool Guide

Keypad Hacker (Tier 1)

Crafting the Tier 1 keypad hacker lets your crew open blue security keypads, including the locks that gate early "special base" rooms. Use this guide to unlock the blueprint, gather the electronics required, and safely deploy the tool on patrol routes across the Office Sector.

Need the quick takeaway?

Crafting this tool is the only way to bypass Level 1 (blue) keypads. Equip it, interact with the pad, and the door unlocks. Higher tiers are required for orange or red locks.

Quick answer

All blue keypads can be opened once the Tier 1 hacker is crafted. The real hurdle is sourcing Security Bot components early and spotting which doors are restricted to Tier 2 or higher.

What you need

  • Unlock the Keypad Hacker (Tier 1) idea.
  • Farm at least one Security Bot CPU to convert into Computation Bricks.
  • Craft the three electronic modules: Controller, LCD Screen, Infrared Emitter.

How to use it

  1. Equip the hacker in a quick-slot.
  2. Interact with a blue keypad.
  3. Remain nearby until the progress bar completes; the door unlatches.

Tier 1 hardware cannot crack orange (Tier 2) or red (Tier 3+) locks.

Prerequisites & unlock paths

Progress through the Office Sector storyline until either the idea triggers or you secure the required bot drop.

Unlocking the blueprint

  • Speak with Alice Mayfield (Office Sector Level 3) after securing the area; several reports credit her with awarding the idea once the dialogue tree completes.1
  • Alternatively, looting a Security Bot CPU and researching it at a station can reveal the same recipe path.2

Bench & skill requirements

  • No specialized job perks are required; any class can craft the modules.
  • Have access to a basic workbench or repair station to assemble electronics.3
  • Stockpile common hardware (screws, circuit boards, metal scrap) in advance to speed through sub-assemblies.

Crafting checklist

These components reflect the most common recipe combination documented across community testing. Names may differ slightly between patches, so confirm in-game before committing rare loot.

If the recipe tree shows duplicate module options, pick the lowest resource path; higher-tier electronics are reserved for later keypad upgrades.

Farming the parts

Security Bots dominate the drop table for the hacker, but clean-up crews can accelerate the process by sweeping electronics-heavy rooms between attacks.

Security Bot routes

  • Tram Plaza: frequent patrols, open sightlines for ranged kiting.1
  • Manufacturing Docks: bots spawn near conveyor belts; expect tight fighting space.2
  • Data Farm corridors: fast respawns but cramped; bring blunt weapons for stagger.

Electronics salvage

  • Raid office cubicles for circuit boards & screws.3
  • Break unused PC towers and terminals after looting data.
  • Keep a fanny pack or cart handy; electronics are weighty but indispensable later.

Special base access

With a Tier 1 hacker crafted, sweep previously locked blue doors in the Office Sector and early safe rooms. Many teams claim their first secure base in these gated labs, so tag them on your map for co-op coordination.

Deploying the hacker in the field

Once crafted, the device behaves like a tool. Treat keypad runs as mini-missions to avoid getting pinned by respawns.

Standard procedure

  1. Clear hostiles around the door before starting the hack.
  2. Activate the hacker; stay within line-of-sight until the progress animation completes.
  3. Listen for the tone — once it dings, open the door and stash the device.

Troubleshooting

  • If the keypad flashes red, verify its tier color; orange locks require Tier 2 hardware.5
  • Blueprint missing? Reload the session or backtrack to the Alice Mayfield encounter; some saves need a trigger reset.5
  • Keep a spare CPU; if a bot despawns mid-fight the drop may vanish until the next cycle.
